# Code of Conduct

---

## Our Goal

Gabida is a technical project, and it is also a community. The goal is to build both well.

We want this to be a space where contributors feel respected, newcomers feel welcomed, and disagreements are resolved through argument rather than friction. The quality of the engine depends on the quality of the collaboration behind it.

We are committed to maintaining a community that is **respectful**, **curious**, **constructive**, and **inclusive** — regardless of experience level, background, or perspective.

---

## Expected Behaviour

- **Respect different opinions.** People bring different experiences and priorities. Disagreement is productive when it is honest and respectful.
- **Criticise ideas, not people.** Challenge a design decision, a PR, or an approach — never the person behind it.
- **Explain your arguments.** In a project with a strong architecture, positions need justification. State your reasoning clearly so others can engage with it.
- **Be welcoming.** A contributor asking their first question deserves the same courtesy as a long-standing maintainer.
- **Assume good intentions.** Most misunderstandings are not malicious. Ask for clarification before assuming bad faith.
- **Help newcomers.** Point people to the right documentation. A contributor who understands the architecture early is a better contributor for everyone.

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Personal attacks or insults directed at contributors, maintainers, or users
- Harassment of any kind, in public or private channels
- Spam, repeated off-topic messages, or deliberate disruption of discussions
- Hostility toward contributors based on experience level, background, or identity
- Discrimination of any kind
- Intentional attempts to derail technical discussions or undermine architectural decisions through bad faith argument

---

## Architecture Discussions

Gabida has an intentional, documented architecture. Disagreements about that architecture are expected and welcome — they are how the project improves.

A few principles specific to architectural discussions:

- **Arguments must be technical.** "I prefer this approach" is a starting point, not an argument. Explain what problem the current approach creates and why the proposed alternative solves it better.
- **Decisions must be justified.** A proposal that cannot be explained in terms of the project's principles — modularity, determinism, testability, maintainability — will be asked for clarification before being considered.
- **Architectural coherence takes precedence over personal preference.** A change that works but breaks the module boundaries is not an improvement. This applies to everyone, including maintainers.
- **Losing an argument is not a failure.** The best outcome of a technical discussion is a clearer understanding of the architecture, whether or not the proposal is accepted.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for upholding this code of conduct. When a violation occurs, maintainers may take any of the following actions depending on the nature and severity of the behaviour:

- **Warning** — a private or public notice that the behaviour is not acceptable
- **Comment removal** — removal of content that violates this code of conduct
- **Discussion lock** — temporary closure of a thread that has become unproductive or hostile
- **Contributor ban** — permanent removal from the project's community spaces for serious or repeated violations

All enforcement decisions are made in good faith and may be contested by contacting the maintainers directly.

---

## Closing

Every contributor who engages honestly and constructively — whether through a bug report, a discussion, a pull request, or a question — helps make Gabida a better narrative engine.

Thank you for being part of that.
